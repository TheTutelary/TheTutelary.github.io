Berlin Trip - Test Post

By Desi European • October 12, 2025

The first thing people tell you about Berlin is that it’s not really Germany. It’s an island—not in the geographical sense, but in the way it breathes, talks, and remembers.

From the Brandenburg Gate to the East Side Gallery, the city is a living history book. Coming here from Sweden, the intensity of the urban energy was a stark contrast to the quiet of Gothenburg, but in the chaotic mix of street art and brutalist architecture, we found a familiar comfort.

The Wall and the Art

Walking along the spree, you see the remnants of the wall. It’s not just concrete; it’s a canvas. The murals don't just depict history—they scream about a future where division is a thing of the past.

The Street Food

While the Currywurst is the legend, the Döner here is the reality. It’s a testament to the city’s immigrant heart, and for a South Asian family, finding that perfect spice levels in a pita bread felt like a small, delicious victory.
